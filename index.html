Dr. Richard Feynman

The Art Behind Science

Here are some of the many accomplishments and milestones from Richard Feynman's life:

  • 1918 - Feynman was born in Queens, New York City.
  • 1933 - At age 15, Feynman taught himself trigonometry, advanced algebra, infinite series, analytic geometry, and both differential and integral calculus.
  • 1942 - Feynman joins the Manhattan Project as the leader of the theoretical division
  • 1965 - Feynman wins the Nobel Prize in physics for "their fundamental work in quantum electrodynamics, with deep-ploughing consequences for the physics of elementary particles."
  • 1986 - After the Space Shuttle Challenger disaster, Feynman was tasked with discovering the root cause. His analysis not only concluded the materials used in the Shuttle's O-rings became less resilient in cold weather, but also uncovered a clear disconnect between Nasa's management and the engineers.
  • 1988 - Richard Feynman passes away due to cancer at the age of 69 in Los Angeles, California.
